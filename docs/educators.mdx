# Educators

DUELink turns abstract coding lessons into hands-on projects: LEDs, sound, sensors, and displays respond as students code. Boards are ready to use—no soldering in the classroom—so you can focus on teaching, not wiring.

**Typical classroom path:** [MicroBlocks](./language/microblocks) on [CincoBit](./products/mccincobit-e) or [PixoBit](./products/mcpixobit-d) → follow the [CincoBit curriculum](./tutorial/cincobit) → optionally [Daisylink](./engine/daisylink) more modules as students advance.

## CS curriculum and lesson plans

An introductory computer science curriculum uses CincoBit and PixoBit. It targets **middle school** classrooms, makerspaces, clubs, and self-paced learners.

Lessons use MicroBlocks to teach programming and computational thinking through graphics, sound, logic, animation, sensors, games, and embedded systems.

The curriculum supports:

* students new to programming
* educators with little or no prior CS teaching experience

Topics include variables, conditions, loops, Boolean logic, lists and arrays, coordinates, sound, functions, and more.

Students can work on **real hardware** or the **Boardie** virtual device for remote and at-home learning.
