
# What is DUELink?

<div class="videocontainer">
  <iframe class="responsive-width"

<br/>

**DUELink** (Dynamic Universal Extensible Link) is a modular electronics system. Each **module** is a small board with its own processor, firmware, and a consistent way to connect and communicate.

You can use modules in two ways:

- **Tethered** — Connect to [supported hardware](./hw/intro)—a [PC or laptop](./hw/pc-laptop), [phone or tablet](./hw/phone-tablet), [Raspberry Pi](./hw/raspberry-pi), [Arduino](./hw/arduino), [micro:bit](./hw/microbit), or [more](./hw/beyond)—over [USB, I2C, UART, or wireless](./interface/intro). Control modules from [Python, JavaScript, .NET, and other languages](./language/intro).
- **Standalone** — Load [MicroBlocks](./language/microblocks), [Arduino](./hw/arduino), or the built-in [scripting engine](./engine/scripting) so the module runs programs without a host.

Chain many modules with **[Daisylink](./engine/daisylink)** on the downlink bus. Mix DUELink with **SparkFun Qwiic**, **Adafruit STEMMA QT**, **Arduino Modulino**, and **Seeed Studio Grove** (with the right adapter). Browse the [product catalog](./catalog/products), or jump to guidance for [engineers](./engineers), [makers](./makers), and [educators](./educators).

## Tethering over USB/I2C/UART
![Daisylink](pathname:///img/daisylink.webp)
</div>

<details>
  <summary>Reach the first module over USB, I2C, or UART—then chain more modules on Downlink.</summary>

The module connected to your host uses [USB, I2C, or serial UART](./interface/intro). Additional modules attach on the [Downlink](./interface/downlink) port and form a **[Daisylink](./engine/daisylink)** chain, so one host connection can control many boards.

</details>

## Internal Scripting Engine
</div>

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center'}}>
```py
x=100
PrintLn(x)
PrintLn("Hello World")
```
</div>

<details>
  <summary>Every module includes a scripting engine—run on the board or command it from a host.</summary>

Each module runs the DUELink [engine](./engine/intro): a [scripting language](./engine/scripting), a large [standard library](./engine/stdlib), plus [graphics](./engine/graphics), a [file system](./engine/filesystem), and more.

Use it **standalone** on the module, or send commands from tethered [hardware](./hw/intro)—a [Raspberry Pi](./hw/raspberry-pi), [micro:bit](./hw/microbit), or [PC](./hw/pc-laptop). You can also load [MicroBlocks](./language/microblocks) or [Arduino](./hw/arduino) firmware when those workflows fit your project better.

</details>

## Qwiic, STEMMA, Modulino
![DUELink STEM I2C qwiic stemmaQT modulino](pathname:///img/qwiic-stemma-modulino-i2c.webp)
</div>


<details>
  <summary>Use SparkFun Qwiic, Adafruit STEMMA QT, Arduino Modulino, and Grove alongside DUELink.</summary>

Ecosystems with I2C JST connectors—**SparkFun Qwiic**, **Adafruit STEMMA QT**, and **Arduino Modulino**—work directly with DUELink modules. **Seeed Studio Grove** devices also work with an adapter cable because the connector differs.

**Two common setups:**

1. Add DUELink modules on the [I2C](./interface/i2c) bus of your [host hardware](./hw/intro), alongside other third-party modules.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center'}}>
![QWIIC, STEMMA QT, and Modulino with DUELink modules](pathname:///img/qwiic-stemma-modulino-duelink.webp)
</div>

2. Wire third-party modules to the [downlink](./interface/downlink) port of a DUELink circuit.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center'}}>
![I2C non-DUELink](pathname:///img/i2c-nonduelink.webp)
</div>

You can combine both approaches in one project.

</details>

## Cableless Green Initiative
![USB Tulips](pathname:///img/usb-tulips.webp)

</div>


<details>
  <summary>Fewer bundled cables—less waste when you already have what you need.</summary>

Individual modules and circuits do not include USB cables; most users already have USB-C cables on hand.

JST cables are also sold separately. [Cable lengths](./products/axcbl) are available when you need them. **DUELink kits include the cables required.**

</details>
